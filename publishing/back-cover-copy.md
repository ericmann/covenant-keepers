# Back Cover Copy — Covenant Keepers Series

*Each blurb is 150-200 words, suitable for the back cover of a printed book.*

## Book 1: The Stone of Courage

Eli and Elena Castillo were supposed to have a quiet summer at their grandfather's house. Instead, they found a hidden room, a mysterious bronze compass called the Pathfinder, and a neighbor named Jordan who doesn't believe in much of anything.

The moment Eli touches the Pathfinder, the three kids are pulled three thousand years into the past — to a valley where an entire army stands frozen in fear and a giant named Goliath dares anyone to fight him.

They already know how the story ends. Everyone does. But standing in the Valley of Elah, close enough to feel the ground shake under Goliath's steps, is nothing like hearing about it in Sunday School. And a strange, creeping fog is rolling in, threatening to erase the courage that changed history forever.

One boy. One stone. One act of faith that echoed through the ages. Can Eli, Elena, and Jordan find the relic before the memory fades — or will the story be lost?

**Word count: 165**

## Book 2: The Branch of Promise

The sky has never been bluer. The village has never been livelier. And the old man building a massive boat on the hill has never been more mocked.

When the Pathfinder sends Eli, Elena, and Jordan to Noah's world, everything looks fine. That's what makes the Fade so dangerous this time — it hides behind perfect weather and common sense. Why build an ark when there isn't a cloud in the sky?

Then the animals begin to arrive. Then God closes the door. And Jordan, the kid who questions everything, finds himself trapped on a boat with a man who gave up his reputation, his neighbors, and his normal life for a promise he couldn't prove.

Inside the Ark, the darkness is absolute. The waiting is unbearable. And faith means holding on when you can't see what you're holding on to.

When the dove finally returns carrying a single olive branch, the kids will learn that God's promises don't expire — even when the whole world says they were never real.

**Word count: 173**

## Book 3: The Thread of Forgiveness

Joseph's brothers threw him in a pit. Then they sat down and ate lunch.

Eli, Elena, and Jordan witness the betrayal firsthand — the torn coat, the slave traders, the blood-soaked lie carried home to a grieving father. It's the ugliest thing the kids have seen in their travels. And Jordan, whose own father walked away, can barely breathe.

Then the Pathfinder jumps forward. Years forward. To Egypt, where the boy from the pit has become the second most powerful man in the empire. And his brothers are kneeling before him, begging for food, with no idea who he is.

The Fade has a new weapon: bitterness. It presses against Joseph's deepest wound, whispering that revenge is justice. That forgiveness is weakness. That some betrayals can never be forgiven.

But at the moment Joseph chooses mercy over vengeance, a single thread from his torn coat begins to glow — and the kids discover that forgiveness isn't about what someone deserves. It's about what kind of person you choose to become.

**Word count: 174**

## Book 4: The Staff of Deliverance

Moses doesn't want to be a hero. He stutters. He argues with God. He begs to be replaced. And Eli Castillo — the kid who always knows the answer but freezes when it's time to speak up — has never related to anyone more.

The Pathfinder drops the three kids into the shadow of the most powerful empire on earth. Pharaoh's word is absolute. Freedom is a fantasy. And the Fade has woven itself into Egypt so deeply that the whole kingdom feels like one enormous weight pressing down on anyone who dares to hope.

Through plagues and darkness and one terrible night, the kids follow a reluctant prophet who slowly learns to trust the voice that called him. And when an entire nation stands trapped between Pharaoh's army and an impossible sea, everything comes down to one man, one wooden staff, and the faith to raise it.

From the phone in Abuelo's hospital room comes a thin, clear voice: "The staff was never magic, mijo. It was just a stick. Remember that."

**Word count: 175**

## Book 5: The Horn of Obedience

March. In silence. For seven days. That's the battle plan.

Elena Castillo has never been patient. She found the hidden room first, she wanted to fight Goliath herself, and she has strong opinions about standing still when there's action to be taken. So when the Pathfinder drops the kids outside the walls of Jericho and the orders come down — walk in circles, blow the horns, don't speak — Elena thinks she might explode.

The Fade arrives earlier than ever before, feeding doubt into the silent ranks. Soldiers whisper about mutiny. The people on the walls jeer. And Elena, her feet bleeding from days of marching, fights the hardest battle of her life: the battle to be still.

But something is shifting in the Covenant Keepers' world. The Fade is growing stronger. Bolder. And the kids are starting to realize that the adventures ahead will demand more from them than ever.

On the seventh day, when the trumpets finally sound and the walls come crashing down, Elena learns that patience is not weakness. Sometimes it's the fiercest kind of courage.

**Word count: 179**

## Book 6: The Seal of Faithfulness

Daniel's enemies searched his entire life for a weakness. The only thing they found was his faithfulness — so they made it a crime.

In the Persian court of Babylon, the Covenant Keepers encounter a new kind of danger. No giants, no floods, no armies. Just politics, jealousy, and a carefully engineered law with one purpose: to destroy a man whose only offense is praying to his God.

The Fade wears its most convincing disguise yet — the voice of reason. Just pray quietly. Just this once. No one would know. It's such a sensible argument that even the kids feel it pulling at them.

Daniel opens his window. He kneels. He prays out loud, toward Jerusalem. As he always has.

In the terrible night that follows — the sealed den, the pacing king, the kids huddled in darkness — something shifts. The Fade is no longer atmosphere. It's putting thoughts directly in their heads. And the realization hits hard: the next six adventures just got a lot more dangerous.

Integrity is not a moment. It's a lifetime.

**Word count: 181**

## Book 7: The Altar Stone

Four hundred and fifty prophets against one. The odds are absurd — and Elijah knows it.

When the Pathfinder drops the kids onto Mount Carmel, they witness a showdown no one should be able to win. An exhausted, terrified prophet challenges an entire nation's false gods to answer with fire. They can't. For hours, the prophets of Baal scream at an empty sky. The silence that answers is the loudest thing the kids have ever heard.

Then Elijah builds an altar. Twelve stones. Drenches it in water three times. And prays — not a performance, just a man talking to the God he believes is real.

Fire from heaven consumes everything. But the Fade isn't trying to erase the fire. It's rewriting what it means: coincidence, weather, luck. For Eli — the careful thinker who's never been the "stand alone" type — this adventure demands the hardest thing he's ever done: choosing truth when the whole crowd disagrees.

When everyone says one thing and the truth says another, which side do you stand on?

**Word count: 172**

## Book 8: The Coin of Second Chances

Jonah knows exactly what God asked him to do. He buys a ticket in the opposite direction.

At the series midpoint, the Pathfinder sends the kids to a harbor where a reluctant prophet is running as fast as he can from his calling. The storm, the sea, the belly of the great fish — it's all terrifying. But the whale isn't the real story. Nineveh is.

When the most wicked city in the ancient world repents — completely, desperately, down to the king himself — Jonah is furious. He wanted them destroyed. They were enemies. And the Fade feeds his rage with a lie so convincing even Jordan feels its pull: they don't deserve mercy.

For Jordan — the skeptic who's been running from faith since the first adventure — Jonah's story hits uncomfortably close. The relic doesn't appear where Jonah arrived. It appears where he ran. Because second chances begin at the moment you admit you're heading the wrong direction.

Seven relics found. Five still waiting. And Jordan just said something that surprised everyone.

**Word count: 175**

## Book 9: The Gleaning Sheaf

No giants. No armies. No fire from heaven. Just a widow gleaning leftover grain in a field — and it might be the bravest thing Elena has ever seen.

After eight adventures full of spectacle and danger, the Pathfinder sends the kids somewhere impossibly quiet: Bethlehem, where two widows arrive with nothing. Ruth bends down and picks up what the harvesters leave behind. Day after day. Backbreaking, invisible, ordinary work.

The Fade changes strategy. No fog, no fear — just a whisper: this story doesn't matter. It's too small. Not worth remembering. And for the first time, Elena almost agrees.

But a landowner named Boaz notices Ruth and quietly leaves extra grain in her path. Small kindness. Enormous cost. And Elena, who has spent the series chasing dramatic moments, discovers that showing up faithfully when no one is watching may be harder than fighting a giant.

The relic appears at the moment of ordinary faithfulness, not dramatic triumph. Because some promises are kept one handful of grain at a time.

**Word count: 170**

## Book 10: The Crown of Courage

A queen hides a secret. A court official signs a death warrant for her entire people. And the only person who can stop it will die if she speaks up.

The Pathfinder pulls the kids into the opulent, deadly Persian court, where Esther has been hiding her identity as a Jew. Haman's decree is sealed — every Jewish man, woman, and child in the empire marked for destruction. Only Esther can intervene. But approaching the king uninvited means death.

The Fade saturates the palace with rational fear: stay quiet, stay safe, someone else will act. It's the most sensible argument the kids have ever heard. Then Mordecai's words cut through everything: "Who knows whether you have come to the kingdom for such a time as this?"

Esther takes the longest walk of her life. The relic appears when she chooses to speak — not when her people are saved. Because the courage to use your voice when silence is safer is the whole point.

Two symbols remain. The endgame is coming.

**Word count: 172**

## Book 11: The Fourth in the Fire

Three young men refuse to bow before a golden statue. The king gives them one more chance. Their answer changes everything Jordan thought he knew about faith.

"Our God is able to deliver us. But even if He does not — we will not bow."

In Babylon, where the Fade has arrived before the kids for the first time, an empire demands total allegiance. The furnace is heated seven times hotter than normal. The soldiers who throw the three men inside don't survive the heat. And then the king sees something impossible: four figures walking in the flames. Not three. Four.

The kids see the fourth figure too. Not rescuing from outside — present inside. Walking through the fire with them. The furnace should have killed them. Instead, it's the most peaceful scene in the entire series.

Jordan, whose entire framework has been "show me proof and I'll believe," has no category for people who hold on without a guarantee. Something fundamental has shifted. He can't name it yet. But he feels it.

One symbol remains.

**Word count: 174**

## Book 12: The Thorn of Promise

A father carries fire and a knife up a mountain. His son carries the wood. There is no lamb.

The final slot on the Pathfinder is empty, and the pull is almost violent. On a dry mountainside, Abraham walks toward the worst moment of his life — and the greatest act of trust in sacred history. The kids know the ending. Everyone does. But living through it is nothing like hearing about it.

For the first time in twelve adventures, the Fade targets the kids directly. Jordan hears whispers about fathers who leave. Elena feels an exhaustion that says this will never end. Eli's need for answers is turned against him. Real pain. Real doubt. The Fade's cruelest weapon.

But when the ram appears in the thicket and Abraham names the place "The Lord Will Provide" — future tense, a promise still unfolding — the completed Pathfinder reveals what twelve adventures have been building: a map of God's faithfulness, every line converging on a center that's still waiting.

The melody is complete. The story is just beginning.

**Word count: 176**
